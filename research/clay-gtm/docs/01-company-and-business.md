# Clay / Claygent Company and Business Research

## One-Line Read

Clay is a GTM data, AI agent, and workflow platform for B2B growth teams. Claygent is its AI web research / GTM agent layer. Clay sells the ability to collect, enrich, reason over, and operationalize go-to-market data, not merely a database or an email tool.

## Target Customers

Clay primarily serves:

- B2B SaaS and technology companies.
- RevOps, Sales Ops, Marketing Ops, Growth, GTM Engineering, ABM, Sales, and Customer Success teams.
- Agencies and consultants that build GTM systems for clients.
- Enterprise teams that need enrichment, CRM maintenance, account research, outbound personalization, ads audiences, and data warehouse workflows.

Public customer references include companies such as OpenAI, Anthropic, Canva, Intercom, Rippling, Cursor, HubSpot, Verkada, Figma, Coverflex, Hex, and Merge.

## Problem It Solves

Clay addresses a common GTM operations problem:

```text
Data is fragmented across CRM, spreadsheets, third-party vendors, websites,
email tools, ad platforms, warehouses, and manual research.
```

Clay brings this into one execution surface:

- Source records from CRM, CSV, webhooks, and list builders.
- Enrich records with multiple data providers.
- Use waterfall logic to maximize coverage and reduce waste.
- Use AI / Claygent to find non-standard public data.
- Score, classify, and personalize outreach.
- Push results to CRM, sequencers, ads, warehouses, Slack, and HTTP APIs.

## Product Positioning

Clay's public positioning has evolved from an intelligent data/workflow tool into a GTM development platform. The important idea is that GTM operators can build, test, and deploy growth workflows without writing a full custom application.

The product category is closer to:

- GTM data operating system.
- GTM workflow development environment.
- Enrichment and AI research table.
- Data marketplace plus action runtime.

It is not simply:

- A CRM.
- A data broker.
- A spreadsheet.
- A cold email sender.
- A general-purpose AI chatbot.

## Pricing Model

Clay uses a hybrid model:

- SaaS subscription tiers.
- Usage-based Data Credits.
- Usage-based Actions.
- Enterprise contracts.
- Data/integration partner ecosystem economics.

Public pricing structure, as observed in June 2026:

| Tier | Public Positioning | Publicly Stated Capacity Signals |
|---|---|---|
| Free | Trial / learning | 100 Data Credits/month, 500 Actions/month, limited table rows. |
| Launch | Individual or small team prospecting workflows | Starts around USD 185/month, includes larger Data Credits and Actions. |
| Growth | Scaling GTM teams | Starts around USD 495/month, adds CRM sync, HTTP API, webhooks, web intent, ads audiences. |
| Enterprise | Large-scale GTM systems | Custom pricing, large credit/action volumes, Clay API, data warehouse sync, SSO, RBAC, dedicated support. |

Data Credits are generally used for marketplace data. Actions meter the work Clay performs, such as enrichment, AI calls, exports, syncs, and workflow execution. BYO API key can reduce Data Credit usage while still consuming Actions.

## Business Model

Clay likely has several revenue layers:

1. Platform subscription.
2. Usage-based orchestration via Actions.
3. Data marketplace economics through Data Credits.
4. Enterprise annual contracts.
5. Ecosystem leverage from agencies, templates, clubs, integrations, and the GTM Engineer category.

## Moat Assessment

Clay's moat is not "an AI agent that can browse." That can be replicated in generic form. The likely moat is the combination:

1. **Data provider network**
   - 150+ publicized providers/sources.
   - Volume pricing and integration reliability.
   - BYO key support.
   - Waterfall rules, caching, and quality feedback.

2. **GTM workflow knowledge**
   - Clay has accumulated specific playbooks for outbound, inbound, CRM enrichment, account research, ABM, signals, CS, retention, and expansion.

3. **Product surface**
   - A table model is familiar to GTM operators.
   - Columns become executable enrichment and AI actions.
   - Results are visible, editable, and auditable.

4. **Enterprise embedding**
   - CRM, warehouse, sequencer, ads, and internal systems create switching costs.

5. **Trust and compliance**
   - Enterprise buyers care about SOC 2, ISO, SSO/RBAC, DPA, subprocessors, and model training commitments.

6. **Category creation**
   - Clay is helping popularize "GTM Engineer" as a role and operating model.

## Legal Replication Opportunity

The safest replication target is:

> A GTM table and AI research system that uses original code, authorized APIs, customer-provided data, public web pages where access is allowed, and compliant data vendors.

Do not replicate:

- Clay brand, UI, copy, icons, customer materials, or proprietary templates.
- Login-only Clay workflows or API behavior.
- Non-public prompts, models, scoring logic, or provider economics.
- Any hidden endpoints discovered from bundles or network inspection.
